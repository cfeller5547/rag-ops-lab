CHAPTER 3: EMPLOYMENT POLICIES
================================================================================

EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY
ACME Corporation is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based
on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic
information, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic.

EMPLOYMENT CLASSIFICATION
- Full-time: Employees scheduled to work 40 hours per week
- Part-time: Employees scheduled to work less than 30 hours per week
- Temporary: Employees hired for a specific project or limited duration

AT-WILL EMPLOYMENT
Employment at ACME Corporation is at-will, meaning either the employee or the
company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without
cause or notice.

BACKGROUND CHECKS
All offers of employment are contingent upon satisfactory completion of a
background check, which may include verification of education, employment
history, criminal records, and credit history where applicable.

IMMIGRATION COMPLIANCE
ACME Corporation complies with all applicable immigration laws and requires
all employees to complete Form I-9 and provide documentation proving their
identity and authorization to work in the United States.

CHAPTER 4: TIME OFF AND LEAVE
================================================================================

PAID TIME OFF (PTO)
Full-time employees accrue PTO based on years of service:
- 0-2 years: 15 days (120 hours) per year
- 3-5 years: 20 days (160 hours) per year
- 6-10 years: 25 days (200 hours) per year
- 10+ years: 30 days (240 hours) per year

PTO accrues on a bi-weekly basis. Unused PTO may be carried over up to a
maximum of 40 hours per year.

REQUESTING TIME OFF
To request time off, employees must:
1. Submit a request through the employee portal at least 2 weeks in advance
2. Obtain approval from their direct supervisor
3. Ensure adequate coverage for their responsibilities during the absence

HOLIDAYS
ACME Corporation observes the following paid holidays:
- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- Presidents' Day
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving Day
- Day after Thanksgiving
- Christmas Eve
- Christmas Day

SICK LEAVE
Full-time employees receive 10 days (80 hours) of sick leave per year.
Sick leave may be used for:
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical appointments
- Care of an immediate family member

PARENTAL LEAVE
ACME Corporation provides:
- Birth parents: 16 weeks of paid parental leave
- Non-birth parents: 8 weeks of paid parental leave
- Adoption/Foster care: 12 weeks of paid leave

Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the birth or placement.

BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
Employees may take paid bereavement leave:
- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent): 5 days
- Extended family (grandparent, sibling, in-law): 3 days

JURY DUTY
Employees called for jury duty will receive their regular pay for up to
10 days per year.

LEAVE OF ABSENCE
Employees may request an unpaid leave of absence for personal reasons.
Requests should be submitted to HR at least 30 days in advance when possible.
Leaves of absence are granted at management discretion.

CHAPTER 5: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS
================================================================================

PAY SCHEDULE
Employees are paid on a bi-weekly basis, every other Friday. Paychecks are
distributed via direct deposit. Employees must provide banking information
through the employee portal to set up direct deposit.

OVERTIME
Non-exempt employees are entitled to overtime pay at 1.5 times their regular
hourly rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. All overtime must be
approved by a supervisor in advance.

HEALTH INSURANCE
ACME Corporation offers comprehensive health insurance plans:
- Medical: PPO and HMO options with varying coverage levels
- Dental: Coverage for preventive, basic, and major dental services
- Vision: Coverage for eye exams, glasses, and contact lenses

The company pays 80% of the premium for employee coverage and 60% for
dependent coverage. Enrollment occurs during annual open enrollment or
within 30 days of a qualifying life event.

RETIREMENT BENEFITS
401(k) Plan:
- Employees may contribute up to the IRS annual limit
- Company matches 100% of contributions up to 4% of salary
- Immediate vesting for employee contributions
- Company match vests over 3 years (33% per year)

To enroll in the 401(k) plan, log in to the employee portal and navigate
to Benefits > Retirement > Enroll.

LIFE INSURANCE
The company provides basic life insurance equal to 1x annual salary at no
cost to employees. Additional voluntary coverage is available for purchase.

DISABILITY INSURANCE
- Short-term disability: 60% of salary for up to 26 weeks
- Long-term disability: 60% of salary after 26 weeks

TUITION REIMBURSEMENT
Full-time employees who have completed one year of service may receive
tuition reimbursement for job-related courses:
- Undergraduate courses: Up to $5,000 per year
- Graduate courses: Up to $7,500 per year

Courses must be approved in advance and a grade of B or better is required
for reimbursement.

EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (EAP)
The EAP provides confidential counseling and support services for employees
and their families, including:
- Mental health counseling
- Financial planning assistance
- Legal consultation
- Work-life balance resources

Contact EAP at 1-800-555-0199 or through the employee portal.

STOCK OPTIONS
Eligible employees may participate in the Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP):
- Purchase company stock at 15% discount
- Contributions via payroll deduction (up to 10% of salary)
- Purchase periods occur quarterly

CHAPTER 6: PERFORMANCE AND DEVELOPMENT
================================================================================

PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
Performance reviews are conducted annually in Q1 for all employees. The
review process includes:
1. Self-assessment completion
2. Manager assessment
3. One-on-one discussion
4. Goal setting for the upcoming year

Mid-year check-ins occur in Q3 to discuss progress and adjust goals as needed.

PROMOTIONS
Promotions are based on:
- Demonstrated performance excellence
- Mastery of current role responsibilities
- Readiness for increased responsibility
- Availability of positions
- Manager recommendation

Employees interested in advancement should discuss career goals with their
manager during performance reviews and development conversations.

INTERNAL TRANSFERS
Employees may apply for internal job postings after completing 12 months in
their current role. To apply:
1. Review open positions on the internal job board
2. Discuss with your current manager
3. Submit application through the employee portal
4. Participate in the interview process

TRAINING AND DEVELOPMENT
ACME Corporation invests in employee development through:
- On-the-job training
- Mentorship programs
- Online learning platform (LinkedIn Learning access)
- Leadership development programs
- Technical certification support

Access learning resources through the employee portal under Development > Learning.

CHAPTER 7: WORKPLACE CONDUCT
================================================================================

CODE OF CONDUCT
All employees are expected to:
- Act with integrity and honesty
- Treat colleagues and customers with respect
- Maintain confidentiality of company information
- Comply with all policies and applicable laws
- Report violations promptly

HARASSMENT POLICY
ACME Corporation maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment of any kind,
including sexual harassment. Harassment includes:
- Unwelcome verbal, physical, or visual conduct
- Conduct that creates an intimidating or hostile environment
- Quid pro quo requests or demands

To report harassment, contact HR, your manager, or use the anonymous
Ethics Hotline at 1-800-555-0123.

CONFLICT OF INTEREST
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with
company interests. Potential conflicts include:
- Financial interests in competitors or vendors
- Outside employment that interferes with job duties
- Personal relationships that could influence decisions

Employees must disclose potential conflicts to their manager and HR.

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
All work created during employment is the property of ACME Corporation.
Employees must:
- Not disclose proprietary information
- Sign confidentiality agreements as required
- Return all company materials upon termination

SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
When using social media:
- Do not share confidential company information
- Clearly identify personal opinions as your own
- Be respectful and professional
- Do not use company logos without permission

OUTSIDE EMPLOYMENT
Employees may engage in outside employment provided it:
- Does not conflict with work schedule
- Does not compete with ACME Corporation
- Does not use company resources
- Is disclosed to and approved by your manager

DRESS CODE
ACME Corporation maintains a business casual dress code. Appropriate attire:
- Professional, neat, and clean appearance
- No clothing with offensive graphics or language
- Client-facing roles may require more formal attire

Remote workers should dress professionally for video calls.

CHAPTER 8: HEALTH AND SAFETY
================================================================================

WORKPLACE SAFETY
Employee safety is our priority. All employees must:
- Follow all safety procedures and guidelines
- Use required protective equipment
- Report unsafe conditions immediately
- Participate in safety training

REPORTING SAFETY CONCERNS
Report safety concerns to:
- Your immediate supervisor
- The Facilities team at facilities@acme.com
- The Safety Hotline at 1-800-555-0156

All reports can be made anonymously.

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
In case of emergency:
1. Follow posted evacuation routes
2. Proceed to designated assembly areas
3. Account for all team members
4. Do not re-enter the building until authorized

Emergency drills are conducted quarterly.

VISITOR POLICY
All visitors must:
- Sign in at the reception desk
- Wear a visitor badge at all times
- Be escorted by an employee
- Sign out upon departure

CHAPTER 9: TECHNOLOGY AND SECURITY
================================================================================

ACCEPTABLE USE
Company technology resources are provided for business purposes. Limited
personal use is permitted provided it:
- Does not interfere with work
- Does not violate any policies
- Does not consume excessive resources

SECURITY REQUIREMENTS
All employees must:
- Use strong, unique passwords
- Enable multi-factor authentication
- Lock workstations when away
- Not share login credentials
- Report suspected security incidents

REPORTING SECURITY INCIDENTS
Report security concerns immediately to:
- IT Security at security@acme.com
- IT Help Desk at 1-800-555-0100
- Your manager

DATA PRIVACY
Employees are responsible for protecting personal and confidential data:
- Only access data necessary for your job
- Do not share data with unauthorized parties
- Follow data retention and disposal policies
- Report data breaches immediately

REMOTE WORK POLICY
Eligible employees may work remotely with manager approval. Remote workers must:
- Maintain a secure home workspace
- Be available during core business hours (9 AM - 3 PM local time)
- Use company-approved tools for communication
- Attend in-person meetings as required

Remote work arrangements are reviewed annually.
